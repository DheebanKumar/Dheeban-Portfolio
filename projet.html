Smart Banana Leaf Disease Classifier Using Convolutional Neural Network

Abstract:

The "Smart Banana Leaf Disease Classifier" employs Convolutional Neural Networks to swiftly and accurately identify diseases in banana plants from leaf images, aiding farmers in proactive disease management and ensuring optimal crop health and yield.

Data:

Classify banana leaves into 13 categories (1 healthy class, 4 diseased classes, and 8 deficient classes) using deep learning models. Utilized images from Mendeley dataset and MUSA dataset, which contained noisy and inconsistent data.

Methodology:

Employed Convolutional Neural Network (CNN) architectures like AlexNet and VGG-16 for classification. Implemented image pre-processing using Single Image Super Resolution (SISR) technique.

Outcome:

Identified AlexNet Model 4 with learning rate 0.0001, epoch of 10, and ADAM optimizer as the suitable model, achieving an accuracy of 96.15%.

Implementation and Integration:

Integration with Dropbox: Stored DL models in Dropbox and generated an access token for app access.

Integration with ThingSpeak: Utilized MATLAB analysis tab in ThingSpeak for downloading trained (.mat) files from Dropbox and resizing input images accordingly. Implemented a classifier to classify the input image and display the class name as output.

Publication: Optimization of AlexNet architecture for Banana Leaf Disease classification, SN Computer Science (under review)

Online Food Ordering System

Abstract:

The "Online Food Order System" is a Java-based application designed to facilitate food ordering and delivery. The system is inspired by popular online food delivery platforms like Swiggy. It provides users with a user-friendly interface to browse through menus, place orders, and provide feedback.

User Registration:

Users can register with the system by providing their address and mobile number. An OTP (One Time Password) verification process ensures security during registration.

Menu Display:

The system displays a menu with various food items and their respective prices, mimicking the offerings of a typical restaurant or food delivery service.

Order Placement:

Users can select items from the menu and add them to their order. The system calculates the total bill dynamically as items are added.

Order Confirmation and Cancellation:

Users have the option to confirm or cancel their order before finalizing it. Confirmation displays the total bill, while cancellation aborts the order process.

Feedback Submission:

After completing an order or at any time during their interaction with the system, users can provide feedback, contributing to the improvement of the service.

Future Enhancements:

Integration with a database to store user details, menu items, and order history. Implementation of user authentication for secure access to the system. Incorporation of real-time order tracking and delivery status updates. Integration with online payment gateways for seamless transactions.

Electronic Dice using Arduino and 7 Segment Display

Abstract:

The Electronic Dice project utilizes Arduino microcontroller technology along with a 7-segment display to create a digital alternative to traditional dice. This project aims to demonstrate the versatility of Arduino in creating interactive and fun electronic devices.

Component Required:

  1. Arduino Uno
  2. 7-segment display
  3. Push-button switches
  4. Resistors
  5. Breadboard
  6. Jumper wires

Random Number Generation:

The Arduino generates random numbers to simulate dice rolls, providing a fair and unbiased result.

Realistic Display:

The 7-segment display accurately represents the numbers rolled, providing a visual indication of the outcome.

User Interaction:

The push-button switches allow users to initiate the dice roll, adding an interactive element to the device.

Compact Design:

The project is designed to be compact and portable, making it suitable for gaming and educational purposes.

RFID Based System for School Children Transportation Safety Enhancement

Project Overview:

The RFID Based System for School Children Transportation Safety Enhancement utilizes Radio-Frequency Identification (RFID) technology to enhance the safety and security of school transportation systems. This project aims to provide a reliable and efficient method for tracking and managing the movement of school children during their commute.

Components Used:

  1. RFID tags/cards: Assigned to each school child for identification.
  2. RFID reader: Installed in school buses to read RFID tags/cards.
  3. Arduino or Microcontroller: To interface with RFID reader and process data.
  4. GPS module: To track the location of school buses.
  5. GSM module: For real-time communication and alerts.
  6. Central database/server: To store and manage student information and bus routes.

Student Identification:

RFID tags/cards uniquely identify each school child, eliminating the possibility of manual errors in attendance tracking.

Automated Attendance:

The system automates the process of taking attendance as students board and leave the bus, reducing administrative workload and improving accuracy.

Real-Time Monitoring:

Administrators and parents can monitor the location and status of school buses and students in real-time using a centralized dashboard or mobile application.

Enhanced Safety:

The system enhances the safety and security of school children during transportation by providing accurate tracking and immediate alerts in case of emergencies.

Applications:

School Transportation: The system is designed to enhance safety and efficiency in school transportation systems, ensuring the well-being of students during their commute.
Parental Communication: Parents can receive notifications and updates about their child's transportation status, providing peace of mind and improving communication between schools and parents.
Emergency Response: In the event of emergencies such as accidents or delays, the system can quickly alert authorities and parents, facilitating timely intervention and support.
